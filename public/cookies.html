Cookie Policy

What cookies FinOwl uses, why, and how to control them. In plain English.

Last updated: 14 April 2026 · UK ICO Compliant

1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites store on your device (computer, phone, or tablet) when you visit them. They are widely used to make websites work properly, remember your preferences, and provide information to site owners.

FinOwl uses cookies and similar technologies (such as localStorage and sessionStorage) to keep you logged in, understand how you use the platform, and respect your preferences.

This policy explains what we use, why, and how to manage or delete them. For more detail on how we handle personal data generally, see our Privacy Policy.

2 Types of Cookies We Use

FinOwl uses three categories of cookies and browser storage:

🔒

Strictly Necessary

Essential for the platform to function. Cannot be disabled. No consent required under ICO rules.

📊

Analytics

Help us understand how users find and use FinOwl. Only set with your consent via the cookie banner.

⚙️

Functional

Remember your preferences and improve your experience. Some require consent, some are strictly necessary.

No advertising cookies. FinOwl does not use advertising, targeting, or third-party tracking cookies. We do not share data with ad networks.

4 Third-Party Cookies

FinOwl loads resources from the following third parties that may set their own cookies:

Google Fonts

We use Google Fonts to serve our typography (Space Grotesk and DM Sans). Google may set cookies or collect technical data (IP address, browser type) when serving fonts. This is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. We are exploring self-hosting fonts to eliminate this third-party request.

Google Analytics (GA4)

Only loaded with your consent. If you accept analytics cookies, Google Analytics 4 is loaded and may set cookies (_ga, _ga_*). These are used to measure aggregate usage patterns. IP addresses are anonymised before transmission to Google. Governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

Stripe

When you visit our checkout page (hosted by Stripe), Stripe may set cookies for fraud prevention and payment processing. These are governed by Stripe's Privacy Policy. These cookies are only set on Stripe's own hosted checkout pages, not on finowl.co.uk itself.

We do not use cookies from social media platforms, advertising networks, or any other third parties not listed here.

5 Your Consent & How to Manage Cookies

Our cookie banner

When you first visit FinOwl's landing page, you will see a cookie consent banner. You can choose:

  • Accept all: We set all cookies described in this policy, including analytics cookies.
  • Decline: Only strictly necessary cookies are set. Analytics cookies are not loaded.

Changing your mind

You can update your cookie preferences at any time by clearing the finowl_cookie_consent value from your browser's localStorage. The cookie banner will reappear on your next visit to the landing page. To do this:

  1. Open your browser's Developer Tools (press F12)
  2. Go to Application → Local Storage → finowl.co.uk
  3. Delete the finowl_cookie_consent entry
  4. Refresh the page — the consent banner will reappear

Browser-level cookie controls

You can control and delete cookies directly through your browser settings. Below are links to instructions for common browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Please note: disabling strictly necessary cookies will prevent FinOwl from working correctly. You will not be able to stay logged in if you block all cookies.

Google Analytics opt-out

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking across all websites by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

6 Legal Basis for Cookies

Our use of cookies complies with the UK's Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) and the UK GDPR, as interpreted by the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

  • Strictly necessary cookies: No consent required under PECR Regulation 6(4). These are essential for the service to function.
  • Analytics and functional cookies: Require your consent under PECR Regulation 6(1). We obtain this via our cookie banner before setting these cookies.

Where we rely on consent, you have the right to withdraw it at any time without detriment. See Section 5 above for how to do this.

If you have a complaint about our use of cookies, you can contact the Information Commissioner's Office:

  • ICO Website: ico.org.uk
  • ICO Helpline: 0303 123 1113

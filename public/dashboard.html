    💬 Frequently Asked Questions

    Only if your VAT-taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 in any 12-month rolling period (2024/25 threshold). Below that, registration is optional. If you're not VAT-registered, you can ignore the VAT section of FinOwl — just use it for income/expense tracking and Self-Assessment.
    MTD is HMRC's programme to move tax filing online. MTD for VAT is already mandatory for all VAT-registered businesses — you must use software to keep records and submit returns. MTD for Income Tax (ITSA) is being phased in from April 2026 for sole traders earning over £50,000/year, and April 2027 for those earning over £30,000. FinOwl is built to be fully MTD-compatible.
    HMRC allows wholly and exclusively business expenses. Common ones: office supplies, business phone/internet (business proportion only), professional fees (accountants, solicitors), advertising and marketing, travel and mileage, professional subscriptions, stock and materials. Not allowed: client entertainment, personal clothing, fines, personal travel. When in doubt, FinOwl flags expenses as "Allowable" or "Disallowable" based on HMRC rules.
    When you connect your bank feed, FinOwl passes each transaction description to an AI model trained to apply HMRC-compliant categories. It returns a category plus a confidence score. High confidence (≥80%) transactions are auto-categorised. Low confidence ones are flagged for your review. You can also create Rules (e.g. "every payment to Amazon = Office Supplies") so the same merchant is always categorised correctly without AI.
    UTR stands for Unique Taxpayer Reference — a 10-digit number HMRC issues when you register for Self-Assessment. You need it to file your tax return and pay your bill. If you're self-employed and haven't got one yet, register on GOV.UK. It can take up to 10 working days. You can enter your UTR in FinOwl under the Self-Assessment tab.
    HMRC requires sole traders to keep business records for at least 5 years after the 31 January submission deadline for that tax year. So for the 2024/25 return (filed by 31 Jan 2026), keep records until at least January 2031. FinOwl stores your categorised transactions securely in the cloud — your records are always there when you need them.
    Yes — most of FinOwl works without VAT registration. Transaction categorisation, expense tracking, mileage, and Self-Assessment all apply to every sole trader. The VAT section will simply show zero liability until you register. You can turn on VAT tracking in Settings when you cross the threshold or choose to register voluntarily.
